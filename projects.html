Assembly Line Layout & Ergonomics Optimization

Organization: Tritorc Manufacturing
Duration: 4 months
Role: Manufacturing Engineer Intern

Project Overview

Redesigned assembly line layout to reduce cycle time, eliminate wasted motion, and improve ergonomic conditions for operators. Combined time-motion studies, spaghetti diagrams, and RULA ergonomic assessments to create optimized workstation configurations.

Problem Analysis

The existing assembly line layout suffered from inefficient material flow and excessive operator movement:

  • Parts storage located far from workstations requiring 85m travel distance per shift
  • Tools scattered across multiple locations causing frequent searching
  • Cycle time of 145 seconds exceeded target of 120 seconds (target takt time)
  • Defect rate of 3.2% primarily caused by assembly errors and rework loops
  • High operator fatigue due to awkward reaching and repetitive motions

Workstation Cycle Time Analysis

Conducted detailed time studies at each workstation to identify bottlenecks and rebalance workflow. The analysis revealed significant opportunities for improvement through line rebalancing and waste elimination.

Ergonomic Assessment & Improvements

Performed RULA (Rapid Upper Limb Assessment) ergonomic evaluations revealing high-risk postures across multiple workstations. Implemented ergonomic improvements including:

  • Height-adjustable work surfaces bringing materials to optimal reach zones
  • Anti-fatigue mats reducing lower back and leg discomfort by 68%
  • Articulating tool balancers eliminating overhead reaching
  • Angled work surfaces maintaining neutral wrist postures
  • Gravity-feed parts presentation reducing repetitive bending motions

Layout Optimization Strategy

  • U-Shaped Cell Design: Converted linear layout to U-shaped configuration reducing travel distance by 45%
  • Point-of-Use Storage: Positioned parts and tools within arm's reach at each workstation
  • Line Balancing: Redistributed work elements achieving ¬±5% balance across stations
  • Visual Indicators: Color-coded parts bins and workflow direction markers
  • One-Piece Flow: Eliminated batch processing reducing WIP inventory by 60%

Cross-Dock Facility Layout Optimization

Organization: DHL Supply Chain
Duration: 3 months
Role: Industrial Engineering Intern

Project Overview

Redesigned cross-dock facility layout to eliminate crossing paths, reduce travel distance, and improve material flow efficiency. Applied facility layout principles and simulation modeling to optimize dock door assignments and staging lane configurations.

Problem Statement

  • Crossing paths causing 12+ congestion incidents per day and safety concerns
  • Average travel distance of 42 meters with dwell time of 4.2 hours per pallet

  • Parallel Flow Patterns: Eliminated cross-traffic by establishing parallel material flow paths
  • Visual Floor Markings: Installed clear directional signage and floor tape for traffic control
  • ABC Analysis: Positioned high-volume SKUs closest to primary dock doors

Poka-Yoke Implementation & Quality System Development

Organization: Tritorc Manufacturing
Duration: 3 months
Role: Manufacturing Engineer Intern

Project Overview

Designed and implemented error-proofing devices (Poka-Yoke) to eliminate defects at the source and reduce quality escapes. Project included root cause analysis, fixture design using CAD, and validation through capability studies.

Problem Identification

Analyzed 6 months of quality data revealing recurring defects in assembly operations:

  • Component misalignment causing 23% of defects
  • Incorrect part orientation accounting for 18% of rework
  • Missing fasteners identified in 15% of quality audits
  • Torque specification errors in critical joints

Poka-Yoke Solutions Implemented

  • Alignment Fixtures: Designed CAD models and fabricated fixtures with keyed pin patterns ensuring only correct component positioning
  • Asymmetric Parts Trays: Created custom-shaped cavities accepting parts in correct orientation only
  • Count-Down Bins: Implemented visual counting system with pre-staged kits preventing missing components
  • Torque-Sensing Tools: Integrated torque wrenches with data logging and visual/audible alerts
  • Go/No-Go Gauges: Designed and implemented inspection gauges at critical control points

Validation & Verification

  • Conducted process capability studies (Cpk analysis) showing improvement from 0.89 to 1.67
  • Performed Gauge R&R studies to validate measurement system effectiveness
  • Monitored SPC control charts for 3 months post-implementation
  • Documented standard operating procedures for new devices
